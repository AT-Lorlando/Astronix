# About

Passionate about science and a lifelong self-learner, I completed a cybersecurity engineering degree while running a web-development micro-business on the side (France's National Student-Entrepreneur Status). Today I'm a DevSecOps engineer at Airbus Defence and Space, through Neverhack, working on European ground-segment systems bound by ESA's requirements.

A hybrid profile: I work across full-stack development, defensive security (SOC, SIEM, vulnerability management), infrastructure and DevSecOps. This triptych (Code, Security, Hardware) gives me a holistic view of modern IT systems — and above all feeds my thirst to learn and build. I'm convinced that understanding the bottom of the stack — network, OS, hardware — makes you better at every level above it.

My homelab is my experimentation ground: a Mikrotik router, a Raspberry Pi (SSH bastion, reverse proxy, internal DNS, home-grown PKI), and a server with two RTX 3090s running my LLMs locally. Before I propose a technology at work, it's already running somewhere at home. That's where my personal projects come from: **Yui** (LLM-driven home automation over MCP), **Hisho** (an AI-augmented portfolio) and **Koya** (a homelab control panel).

Away from the screen: astronomy, aviation, climbing and photography.

In short, a hybrid profile who can both quit Vim and center a div.
